Introduction

Through this project, the research team plans to explore the viability of location history (LH) data to model individual travel patterns. Using LH data, the project team plans to develop machine learning (ML)-based predictive models to forecast travel behavior of individuals. LH data (coupled with a small survey to solicit socio-demographic information) will be collected from a small sample of individuals through emTripLog, an open-sourced smart phone application.

Purpose

LH data coupled with socio-demographic info will be used to develop a suite of generic ML-based predictive models for forecasting:
  1. ✔️ Activity-type choice, i.e., what types of activities will an individual participate in during a day
  2. ✔️ Departure time choice, i.e., at what time the individual start traveling to each activity
  3. ✔️ Mode choice, i.e., which mode an individual used to travel to an activity

What we collect

By participating in this program, you agree to allow us to track metrics pertaining to your location, accelerometer, device-generated activity and mode recognition, app usage time and battery usage. We will also ask that you enter your experiences and feedback during daily surveys. Furthermore, there will be an initial survey to collect your sociodemographic information. We would like to assure you that your information will never be made public or sold, and is collected only for the purposes of this project without any commercial third party involvement. We use an open source platform, e-mission and the associated emTripLog app, for the data collection. You can inspect the data that e-mission collects in the background at https://github.com/e-mission/e-mission-data-collection.git, and view the configuration for this pilot at https://github.com/e-mission/e-mission-phone/tree/nrel_location_history

How we associate it with you

Each participant’s phone will be associated with a randomly generated token that is stored on the server on registration. Nobody other than the participant will have access to the relationship between the token key and the participant. This means that if you switch phones or uninstall and reinstall the app, you will not have access to your prior data.

Who gets to see it

Only researchers working on this project will have access to the raw data. Data collected through this project will not be shared with anyone outside the core research team. Any research related requests for processed data will be handled on a case-by-case basis, and even in the case that data is shared with outside researchers, only aggregated and anonymized versions of the data will be shared.

Your rights

Participation in research is completely voluntary. You have the right to decline to participate or to withdraw at any point in this study without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.
